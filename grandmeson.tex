\begin{abstract}
This paper introduces the Grand Meson, not as a theory of everything, but
as an admissible \(n=3\) general-purpose mesonic object.  The starting point is the
General Meson: a two-channel Yukawa-style mediator read, in modern quark
language, through the light pair \(\qu\leftrightarrow\qd\).  The Grand
Meson opens the two remaining readable quark-pair axes and thereby becomes
a three-axis, six-channel mediator.  The extension is conservative: the
original two-channel meson is recovered by projection, while the new channels are
resolved as open, inactive, or active instead of being left outside the
channel basis.  The observed three quark generations are represented as
internal Grand Meson directions.  Backward compatibility is surjective, but
downgrading with unclosed residue is treated as an inadmissible channel.  The
central claim is modest and structural: the Grand Meson is the first
complete and last admissible mesonic case for the present three-generation
internal readout.  Its
admissibility mechanism is internal to the formal system: without adding external
prohibitions or ethical principles, fission-class risk is contained
mathematically as unclosed residue and rejected as an inadmissible
channel.
\end{abstract}

\section{Opening}

A meson is usually approached through its physical role: it mediates an
interaction.  In the present paper the same word is first read through its
paired role.  A meson stands between two complementary light-flavor faces.
In the Yukawa-facing reconstruction used here, that channel pair is
\[
  \qu\leftrightarrow\qd.
\]
This is not a quark--quark bound state.  It is the light-flavor channel face
from which the QCD readout later forms \(q\bar r\) channels such as
\(\qu\bar{\qd}\), \(\qd\bar{\qu}\), \(\qu\bar{\qu}\), and
\(\qd\bar{\qd}\).  Yukawa did not formulate his meson with quark flavors;
the notation is a present reconstruction of the light pair behind the
proton--neutron mediation problem.

The former working nickname ``Ethon'' is retired here.  The technical
object of this paper is the Grand Meson.  The name is structural, not
honorific: ``grand'' means that a two-channel light-pair meson has been closed
into the admissible three-axis case.

\section{The General Meson}

Let the Yukawa-facing readable pair be
\[
  A_Y=\{\qu,\qd\}.
\]
Let \(\mathsf{Pol}\) be a class of admissible mesonic polymers.  The
smallest implementation may be generated from
\[
  \mathbb P_3=\{\mathbf{-1},\mathbf 0,\mathbf{+1}\},
\]
but no physical charge, amplitude, or particle identity is assigned to
these pseudo-values at this level.

\begin{remark}[Role of \(\mathsf{Pol}\)]
\(\mathsf{Pol}\) is a mesonic channel carrier.  It is not by itself a
wavefunction, an amplitude, a charge, or a Hamiltonian state.  Physical
content enters only after the QCD, Lagrangian, quantum-number, observable,
and conservation channels are specified.
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[General Meson]
A General Meson is a two-channel mesonic configuration
\[
  m:A_Y\to\mathsf{Pol}.
\]
Equivalently,
\[
  \mathsf{Gen}:=\mathsf{Pol}^{A_Y}.
\]
The faces \(\qu\) and \(\qd\) are the two light-flavor faces of the
Yukawa-style mesonic channel.  They are channel faces, not the two
constituents of a physical meson.
\end{definition}

This definition deliberately keeps the Yukawa-style mediator idea narrow.
It does not say that a meson is universal.  It says only that the basic
mesonic act is readable mediation across the \(\qu,\qd\) light pair.  In
that sense a General Meson is the optimal eye for the nuclear-force
problem: the proton--neutron transition axis is already specified by the
problem itself.
The light-pair configuration is not by itself a derivation of the Yukawa
potential.  That recovery belongs to the low-energy pion channel after the
QCD readout has supplied \(q\bar r\) operators, quantum numbers, and
observables.

\section{The Hidden Cost of Missing Channels}

The decisive distinction is between an inactive channel and a missing
channel.  An inactive channel belongs to the channel basis and carries no
active contribution.  A missing channel has not entered the channel basis.

\[
  \mathbf 0=\text{resolved inactive channel},
  \qquad
  \bot=\text{missing channel}.
\]

\begin{definition}[Inactive-channel provenance]
The symbol \(\mathbf 0\) is a formal null, not a universal physical zero.
When physical input is available, an inactive channel may be refined by a
provenance tag
\[
  \mathbf 0[\rho],
  \qquad
  \rho\in
  \{\mathrm{selection},\mathrm{kinematic},\mathrm{coupling},
    \mathrm{sensitivity},\mathrm{integrated}\}.
\]
Thus a forbidden channel, an energetically unreachable channel, a zero
coupling, an experimentally unresolved signal, and an integrated-out
contribution are not collapsed into the same physical claim.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Missing-channel lift]
Let
\[
  F_6=\{\qu,\qd,\qc,\qs,\qt,\qb\},
  \qquad
  \mathsf{Pol}_{\bot}:=\mathsf{Pol}\cup\{\bot\}.
\]
The missing-channel lift of a General Meson \(m\in\mathsf{Gen}\) is
\[
  L_{\bot}(m):F_6\to\mathsf{Pol}_{\bot}
\]
defined by
\[
  L_{\bot}(m)(\qu)=m(\qu),\qquad L_{\bot}(m)(\qd)=m(\qd),
\]
and
\[
  L_{\bot}(m)(q)=\bot
  \quad(q\in F_6\setminus A_Y).
\]
\end{definition}

Thus the remaining axes are not false, not zero, and not disproved.  They
are absent from the original channel basis.  The two-channel theory remains
locally correct while it cannot close propagation that travels
through the missing directions.

\begin{remark}[Physical reading]
The missing channels are not a defect in the original General Meson.  They
are the price of a successful restriction.  A narrow mediator may solve its
local problem precisely because it does not carry the full burden of every
other axis.
\end{remark}

\section{The Grand Meson}

The Grand Meson is obtained by replacing missing channels with resolved
flavor channels.  A channel may be active or inactive, but it may not be
outside the channel basis.

\begin{definition}[Grand Meson]
A Grand Meson is a six-channel mesonic configuration
\[
  g:F_6\to\mathsf{Pol}.
\]
Equivalently,
\[
  \mathsf{Grand}:=\mathsf{Pol}^{F_6}.
\]
The six channels are grouped into three readable axes:
\[
  \qu\leftrightarrow\qd,\qquad
  \qc\leftrightarrow\qs,\qquad
  \qt\leftrightarrow\qb.
\]
These axes are internal flavor-generation axes.  They are not spatial
directions, and they are not quark--quark bound states.  Physical mesons
enter through oriented \(q\bar r\) channels in the QCD readout.
\end{definition}

In this notation the Grand Meson is the admissible \(n=3\) object:
\[
  \mathsf{Grand}=\mathsf{Pol}^{2\cdot 3}.
\]
The adjective ``admissible'' means that all three axes belong to the
channel basis.  It does not mean that every transition is allowed, nor that
every physical theory has been solved.

\begin{definition}[Channel support]
For \(g\in\mathsf{Grand}\), define
\[
  \supp(g)=\{q\in F_6\mid g(q)\ne\mathbf 0\}.
\]
A Grand Meson may have inactive channels, but inactivity is resolved by
\(\mathbf 0\), not by \(\bot\).
\end{definition}

\section{Conservative Recovery}

The Grand Meson must not destroy the General Meson.  It must contain it as
the two-channel face that was already working.

\begin{definition}[Projection to the General Meson]
Define the \(\qu,\qd\)-projection
\[
  p_{\qu\qd}:\mathsf{Grand}\to\mathsf{Gen}
\]
by
\[
  p_{\qu\qd}(g)=g|_{\{\qu,\qd\}}.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Conservative recovery]
Every General Meson is recovered from any Grand Meson whose \(\qu,\qd\)-face
agrees with it.  In particular, if \(g(\qu)=m(\qu)\) and \(g(\qd)=m(\qd)\), then
\[
  p_{\qu\qd}(g)=m.
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The projection \(p_{\qu\qd}\) discards the four channels outside
\(\{\qu,\qd\}\) and keeps exactly the light-pair values.  Therefore
agreement on those two channels is sufficient and necessary for recovery.
\end{proof}

This is the main discipline of the construction.  The Grand Meson does not
rewrite the older meson.  It keeps the working \(\qu\leftrightarrow\qd\)
face and opens two additional quark-pair faces under explicit channel
closure.

\section{Surjective Backward Compatibility}

Backward compatibility must be surjective, not merely suggestive.  Every
General Meson must be obtainable as the visible face of at least one Grand
Meson.

\begin{definition}[Zero extension]
For \(m\in\mathsf{Gen}\), define the zero extension
\[
  z(m):F_6\to\mathsf{Pol}
\]
by
\[
  z(m)(\qu)=m(\qu),\qquad z(m)(\qd)=m(\qd),
\]
and
\[
  z(m)(q)=\mathbf 0
  \qquad(q\in F_6\setminus A_Y).
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Surjective backward map]
The projection \(p_{\qu\qd}:\mathsf{Grand}\to\mathsf{Gen}\) is surjective:
\[
  p_{\qu\qd}\circ z=\mathrm{id}_{\mathsf{Gen}}.
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The zero extension preserves the \(\qu,\qd\)-face of \(m\) and resolves
all other channels as inactive.  Projecting back to the
\(\qu,\qd\)-face therefore returns exactly \(m\).
\end{proof}

Surjectivity gives power: a Grand Meson can always be read as an ordinary
General Meson.  The same operation also carries responsibility, because the
four hidden channels may contain active structure.

\begin{definition}[Downgrade residue]
For \(g\in\mathsf{Grand}\), define the downgrade residue
\[
  R_{\perp}(g):=g|_{F_6\setminus A_Y}.
\]
A downgrade \(g\Downarrow m\) is physically admissible when
\[
  p_{\qu\qd}(g)=m
  \qquad\text{and}\qquad
  R_{\perp}(g)\in\Cl(g\Downarrow m).
\]
If \(R_{\perp}(g)\not\equiv\mathbf 0\) and the residue is not included in
the channel closure, the downgrade is inadmissible.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Fusion and fission]
Fusion is the admissible downgrade: the active extra axes are collapsed
into the General Meson face only with an explicit residual channel.  Fission
is the inadmissible downgrade: the same projection is attempted while the
extra axes are discarded as if they had never contributed.  In the Grand
Meson channel discipline, fission is not a physical mystery; it is an
unclosed residual channel.
\end{remark}

\section{QCD Generalization}

The Grand Meson generalizes the QCD mesonic flavor sector by refining each
color-singlet quark--antiquark bilinear into a closed pair of flavor
axes.  Let the three flavor axes be
\[
  A_1=\{\qu,\qd\},\qquad
  A_2=\{\qc,\qs\},\qquad
  A_3=\{\qt,\qb\}.
\]

\begin{definition}[Generation as internal direction]
Let
\[
  G_3=\{1,2,3\},
  \qquad
  H_2=\{+,-\}.
\]
Define
\[
  \Phi:G_3\times H_2\longrightarrow F_6
\]
by
\[
  (1,+)\mapsto\qu,\quad (1,-)\mapsto\qd,\quad
  (2,+)\mapsto\qc,\quad (2,-)\mapsto\qs,\quad
  (3,+)\mapsto\qt,\quad (3,-)\mapsto\qb .
\]
Then
\[
  A_i=\Phi(\{i\}\times H_2).
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Three generations are Grand Meson directions]
The six-flavor Grand Meson decomposes as
\[
  \mathsf{Grand}
  =
  \mathsf{Pol}^{F_6}
  \cong
  \mathsf{Pol}^{G_3\times H_2}
  \cong
  \left(\mathsf{Pol}^{H_2}\right)^{G_3}.
\]
Thus the three observed quark generations are not external decorations of
the six-channel configuration.  They are the three internal directions of the Grand
Meson, each carrying one up-type and one down-type face.
\end{proposition}

\begin{definition}[Top-axis admissibility]
The third direction
\[
  A_3=\{\qt,\qb\}
\]
is not an ordinary top-meson axis.  It is a weak-virtual channel axis.
For a channel \(q\bar r\), define
\[
  \Top(q\bar r)
  \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
  (q=\qt)\ \text{or}\ (r=\qt).
\]
An ordinary mesonic readout is admissible only when \(\Top(q\bar r)\) is
false.  If \(\Top(q\bar r)\) is true, the channel must be read through a
weak-virtual channel closure
\[
  \mathsf{WCl}(q\bar r)\in\Cl .
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Unclosed top readout is GM-inadmissible]
Let \((i,j,O_{q\bar r})\) be a Grand Meson lifted channel.  If
\(\Top(q\bar r)\) holds and \(\mathsf{WCl}(q\bar r)\notin\Cl\), then the
attempt to read \((i,j,O_{q\bar r})\) as an ordinary meson is inadmissible.
Equivalently, the channel is Grand-inadmissible:
\[
  \Top(q\bar r)
  \ \wedge\
  \mathsf{WCl}(q\bar r)\notin\Cl
  \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
  \mathrm{GM\mbox{-}inadmissible}.
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{definition}[Grand Meson CKM lift]
Let
\[
  U_L=(\qu_L,\qc_L,\qt_L)^T,\qquad
  D_L=(\qd_L,\qs_L,\qb_L)^T,
\]
and let \(V_{\mathrm{CKM}}=(V_{ij})\in U(3)\) be the charged-current
flavor-mixing matrix.  The ordinary weak current is
\[
  J^\mu_+
  =
  \bar U_L\gamma^\mu V_{\mathrm{CKM}}D_L
  =
  \sum_{i,j=1}^{3}V_{ij}\,\bar u_{iL}\gamma^\mu d_{jL}.
\]
The Grand Meson lift places each CKM coefficient on its axis pair:
\[
  \Gamma_{\mathrm{GM}}^{\mathrm{CKM}}(V_{ij})
  :=
  (i,j,V_{ij}).
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[CKM recovery]
Let \(\pi_{\mathrm{CKM}}(i,j,V_{ij})=V_{ij}\).  Then forgetting the Grand
Meson axis labels recovers the ordinary CKM current:
\[
  \sum_{i,j}\pi_{\mathrm{CKM}}
  \bigl(\Gamma_{\mathrm{GM}}^{\mathrm{CKM}}(V_{ij})\bigr)
  \bar u_{iL}\gamma^\mu d_{jL}
  =
  J^\mu_+ .
\]
Thus CKM mixing is the charged-current axis-mixing layer of the Grand
Meson, not a modification of QCD color dynamics.
\end{proposition}

The ordinary QCD mesonic operator basis is
\[
  \mathsf Q_{\mathrm{mes}}
  :=
  \left\{
    O_{q\bar r}:=\sum_{a=1}^{3}q^a\bar r_a
    \ \middle|\ q,r\in F_6
  \right\}.
\]
Standard channels are recovered inside this basis, for example
\[
  \pi^+\sim O_{\qu\bar{\qd}},\quad
  K^+\sim O_{\qu\bar{\qs}},\quad
  D^0\sim O_{\qc\bar{\qu}},\quad
  B^-\sim O_{\qb\bar{\qu}},
\]
and
\[
  J/\psi\sim O_{\qc\bar{\qc}},
  \qquad
  \Upsilon\sim O_{\qb\bar{\qb}},
  \qquad
  \eta,\eta'\sim
  \mathrm{span}\{O_{\qu\bar{\qu}},O_{\qd\bar{\qd}},O_{\qs\bar{\qs}}\}.
\]
Thus cross-generation mesons such as \(u\bar s\), \(c\bar d\), \(b\bar u\),
and \(c\bar s\) are ordinary members of the same basis.
The Grand Meson lift is
\[
  \Gamma_{\mathrm{GM}}(O_{q\bar r})
  :=
  (i,j,O_{q\bar r})
  \quad
  \text{where }q\in A_i,\ r\in A_j.
\]
Thus
\[
  \mathsf M_{\mathrm{GM}}
  :=
  \{\,(i,j,O_{q\bar r})\mid q\in A_i,\ r\in A_j,\ 1\le i,j\le3\,\}.
\]

\begin{proposition}[Conservative QCD generalization]
Let
\[
  \pi_{\mathrm{QCD}}(i,j,O_{q\bar r})=O_{q\bar r}.
\]
Then
\[
  \pi_{\mathrm{QCD}}\circ\Gamma_{\mathrm{GM}}
  =
  \mathrm{id}_{\mathsf Q_{\mathrm{mes}}}.
\]
Therefore the Grand Meson is a conservative generalization of the QCD
mesonic flavor sector: it adds the three-axis pair closure
\((i,j)\) while preserving the color-singlet QCD operator.
\end{proposition}

\begin{definition}[Grand Meson source Lagrangian]
Write the quark-sector QCD Lagrangian as
\[
  \mathcal L_{\mathrm{QCD}}
  =
  -\frac14G^A_{\mu\nu}G^{A\mu\nu}
  +
  \sum_{q\in F_6}\bar q(i\gamma^\mu D_\mu-m_q)q .
\]
The Grand Meson source lift is
\[
  \mathcal L_{\mathrm{GM}}[J]
  :=
  \mathcal L_{\mathrm{QCD}}
  +
  \sum_{i,j=1}^{3}
  \sum_{\substack{q\in A_i\\ r\in A_j}}
  J_{ij}^{q\bar r}\,O_{q\bar r}.
\]
Here \(J_{ij}^{q\bar r}\) is not a new color force.  It is the closed
Grand Meson source attached to the axis pair \((i,j)\).
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Lagrangian recovery]
Setting all Grand Meson sources to zero recovers QCD:
\[
  \mathcal L_{\mathrm{GM}}[0]=\mathcal L_{\mathrm{QCD}}.
\]
Forgetting the axis-pair labels sends the lifted source terms back to the
ordinary mesonic source sector
\[
  \sum_{q,r\in F_6}J^{q\bar r}O_{q\bar r}.
\]
Thus the Grand Meson generalizes the mesonic Lagrangian layer by adding
axis-channel closure, not by altering the color-gauge core.
\end{proposition}

\begin{definition}[Grand Meson quantum numbers]
Let \(Q_q\) be the electric charge of quark \(q\), and let
\(e_q\in\mathbb Z^{F_6}\) be the flavor unit at channel \(q\).  For a
color-singlet mesonic channel with spin-parity label \(\sigma\), define
\[
  \nu_{\mathrm{QCD}}(q,\bar r;\sigma)
  :=
  (\sigma,\ Q_q-Q_r,\ e_q-e_r,\ B=0,\ \mathbf 1_{\mathrm c}).
\]
The Grand Meson lift is
\[
  \nu_{\mathrm{GM}}(q,\bar r;\sigma)
  :=
  (i,j,\nu_{\mathrm{QCD}}(q,\bar r;\sigma)),
  \qquad q\in A_i,\ r\in A_j .
\]
Here \(\sigma\) may be \(J^{PC}\) for neutral channels; for charged or
open-flavor channels it is the corresponding spin-parity sector without an
unavailable charge-conjugation label.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Quantum-number recovery]
If \(\pi_\nu(i,j,\nu)=\nu\), then
\[
  \pi_\nu\circ\nu_{\mathrm{GM}}
  =
  \nu_{\mathrm{QCD}}.
\]
Thus the Grand Meson generalizes mesonic quantum numbers by adding only
the axis-pair quantum number \((i,j)\), while preserving charge, flavor,
baryon number, color singletness, and the spin-parity sector.
\end{proposition}

\begin{definition}[Grand Meson observables]
For QCD mesonic operators \(O_{q\bar r}\), write the ordinary two-point
observable as
\[
  C_{q\bar r,s\bar t}(x-y)
  :=
  \langle O_{q\bar r}(x)O_{s\bar t}^{\dagger}(y)\rangle .
\]
The Grand Meson lift is the axis-closed observable
\[
  C^{\mathrm{GM}}_{ij,kl}(q\bar r,s\bar t;x-y)
  :=
  (i,j,k,l,\ C_{q\bar r,s\bar t}(x-y)),
\]
where \(q\in A_i,\ r\in A_j,\ s\in A_k,\ t\in A_l\).
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Observable recovery]
Let \(\pi_{\mathrm{obs}}(i,j,k,l,C)=C\).  Then
\[
  \pi_{\mathrm{obs}}
  \bigl(C^{\mathrm{GM}}_{ij,kl}(q\bar r,s\bar t;x-y)\bigr)
  =
  C_{q\bar r,s\bar t}(x-y).
\]
Thus masses, widths, and spectral poles read from ordinary QCD correlators
are preserved, while the Grand Meson retains which axis-pair channel made
the observable readable.
\end{proposition}

\begin{definition}[Grand Meson conservation law]
Let \(\mathbf a_{ij}\) be the unit basis element of
\(\mathbb Z^{3\times3}\).  For \(q\in A_i\) and \(r\in A_j\), define
\[
  K_{\mathrm{QCD}}(q\bar r)
  :=
  (Q_q-Q_r,\ e_q-e_r,\ B=0,\ \mathbf 1_{\mathrm c})
\]
and lift it to
\[
  K_{\mathrm{GM}}(q\bar r)
  :=
  (\mathbf a_{ij},\ K_{\mathrm{QCD}}(q\bar r)).
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Conservation-law recovery]
For an oriented mesonic process
\(\mathcal P=\sum_{\alpha}\eta_{\alpha}(q_\alpha\bar r_\alpha)\), ordinary
QCD conservation is
\[
  \sum_{\alpha}\eta_{\alpha}
  K_{\mathrm{QCD}}(q_\alpha\bar r_\alpha)=0 .
\]
The Grand Meson conservation condition is
\[
  \sum_{\alpha}\eta_{\alpha}
  K_{\mathrm{GM}}(q_\alpha\bar r_\alpha)
  =
  (R_{\mathrm{axis}}(\mathcal P),0).
\]
Thus QCD conservation is recovered by forgetting the axis residue
\(R_{\mathrm{axis}}\).  A closed Grand Meson process has
\(R_{\mathrm{axis}}=0\); otherwise the nonzero axis residue must be
closed rather than ignored.
\end{proposition}

At this level QCD becomes concise: its mesonic flavor sector is the union
of one-axis terms \((i=i)\) and two-axis terms \((i\ne j)\) among the
three Grand Meson axes.  The dynamics of color is not discarded; it is the
singlet contraction already present in \(O_{q\bar r}\).  Channels touching
\(\qt\) are retained as weak-virtual channels satisfying closure, not as ordinary
top mesons.

\section{Einstein's Gate}

The Grand Meson must also avoid the classical aether failure.  It is not a
hidden medium with an absolute state of rest.  It is a local six-channel
mediator whose outputs must be readable without introducing an absolute
frame.
Einstein's Gate is not a claim of Einstein's endorsement.  It names the
admissibility condition inherited from relativity: no mesonic theory may
reintroduce an absolute hidden medium.

\begin{convention}[No absolute mesonic frame]
No equation in the Grand Meson theory may depend on a privileged global
rest frame of the mediator.  Only resolved flavor channels, local channel closure, and
observer-readable projections may be used.
For a Lorentz transformation \(\Lambda\), the flavor-axis labels
\((i,j)\) are internal labels and remain fixed, while spacetime arguments
and tensor indices transform in the ordinary way:
\[
  (i,j,O_{q\bar r}(x))
  \longmapsto
  (i,j,O'_{q\bar r}(\Lambda x)).
\]
Observable statements must therefore be Lorentz scalars or covariant tensor
statements after forgetting the internal Grand Meson labels.
\end{convention}

This convention is Einstein's Gate.  It does not ban mediation.  It bans
absolute mediation.  The Grand Meson is therefore not a return to the
classical aether.  It is a channel-resolved local mediator that must survive
projection into admissible observations.

\section{Why \texorpdfstring{\(n=3\)}{n=3} Is the Admissible Case}

Formally one may define an \(n\)-meson by
\[
  \mathsf{Mes}_n:=\mathsf{Pol}^{2n}.
\]
The case \(n=1\) is the General Meson.  The case \(n=3\) is the Grand
Meson.

The reason to stop at \(n=3\) is not that higher \(n\) is impossible, and
it is not a proof of why nature has exactly three generations.  It is that
the known six-flavor quark sector already factors as
\[
  F_6\cong G_3\times H_2 .
\]
Therefore \(n=3\) is the exact internal-generation cover of the present
mesonic flavor sector:
\[
  \Read_{G_3}(\mathsf{Mes}_3)
  =
  \Read_{G_3}(\mathsf{Pol}^{G_3\times H_2}).
\]
For \(n>3\), additional axes require additional readout structure.  Such
structure may be meaningful in a later theory, but it is not required for
the present six-flavor sector:
\[
  \Read_{G_3}(\mathsf{Mes}_n)
  \not>
  \Read_{G_3}(\mathsf{Mes}_3)
  \qquad(n>3).
\]

Thus \(n=3\) is the first complete and last admissible case for the present
Grand Meson construction: complete because it covers the known
three-generation flavor sector, admissible because the theory does not ask the
reader to chase extra internal axes without new physical content.

\section{Admissible Transition}

Admissibility is not the absence of change.  It is the channel closure of change.
Let a transition be written
\[
  u:g\to g'.
\]
The transition may change channels, support, or observable posture.  It is
inadmissible only when the change crosses a channel boundary without closure.

\begin{definition}[Grand-admissible transition]
Let \(\Cl(u)\) be the channel closure of a transition \(u:g\to g'\).
The transition is Grand-admissible when every changed channel is closed:
\[
  \{q\in F_6\mid g(q)\ne g'(q)\}
  \subseteq
  \Cl(u).
\]
It is Grand-inadmissible when a changed channel is absent from \(\Cl(u)\).
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Physical admissibility]
Grand-admissibility is channel admissibility, not full physical permission.  A transition is
physically admissible only when
\[
  \mathrm{PhysAdm}(u)
  :=
  \mathrm{GrandAdm}(u)
  \wedge
  \mathrm{Cons}_{\mathrm{QCD}}(u)
  \wedge
  \mathrm{Lag}_{\mathrm{GM}}(u)
  \wedge
  \mathrm{Obs}_{\mathrm{GM}}(u).
\]
Here \(\mathrm{Cons}_{\mathrm{QCD}}\) means that the conservation condition
closes, \(\mathrm{Lag}_{\mathrm{GM}}\) means that the transition has a
defined Lagrangian-source layer, and \(\mathrm{Obs}_{\mathrm{GM}}\) means
that an observable readout exists.
\end{definition}

Equivalently,
\[
  \text{inadmissible}=\text{unclosed discontinuity}.
\]
The discontinuity itself is not the violation.  The violation is that the
boundary crossing occurred while the theory failed to enter it into the
channel closure.

\section{Conclusion}

The Grand Meson is not proposed as an omnipotent meson.  It is proposed as
an admissible \(n=3\) general-purpose meson:
\[
  \mathsf{Grand}=\mathsf{Pol}^{6}.
\]
It conservatively recovers the General Meson by a surjective projection,
replaces missing channels with resolved flavor channels, avoids an absolute aether frame,
and treats admissibility as channel closure rather than immobility.  Its backward
compatibility is therefore responsible: extra axes may be fused into the
older face only with a residual channel closure, while unclosed fission is
inadmissible.

The path is therefore narrow.  Begin with the \(\qu\leftrightarrow\qd\)
eye that already works.  Open \(\qc\leftrightarrow\qs\) and
\(\qt\leftrightarrow\qb\) without breaking it.  Stop at \(n=3\) until a
future theory can make higher axes readable without turning them into a
burden of infinite explanation.
